Bird Biography

Black-rumped Flameback (Old names - Lesser Goldenback)

Length: 26–29 cm.
Size: Larger than the Common Myna.
Identification: Wings are golden and the rump and lower back is black.Primaries are barred. The neck is black with white dots on it. There is a black bandage through the eye and there is a lack of moustache. The crown of the male is red, while the female crown is black on the top and red on the top. Climbs on tree trunks, tapping and chiselling the bark or wood.
Voice: Loud harsh 'clerk' as well as ‘key-key-key' somewhat like laughing.
Residential status: Resident throughout India.
Habitat: Open forests, groves of old trees, coconut plantations. Diet: Insectivorous. Insects, like beetles, termites hiding under the bark or in rotting tree trunks. Occasionally alights on the ground to feed on black ants. Fruit pulp as well as flower nectar. Occasionally seen feeding on grains, cooked food and even ‘Samosas’ offered by humans.
Breeding:March to August.
