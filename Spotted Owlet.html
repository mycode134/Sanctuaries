Bird Biography

Spotted Owlet Athene brama.

Length: 21 cm.
As of Common Myna.
Identification: Small owlet with yellow eyes and no ear tufts. Heavily spotted above and scaled below. Has pale facial disc and pale hind collar. Dark cheeks are enclosed by white border. Tail narrowly banded. Crepuscular in habit as it is active in early morning, late evening and in night.
Voice: Calls are harsh and screechy ‘chirurr… chirurr…chirurr’ and ‘cheevak…cheevak’.
Residential status: Resident. Throughout India.
Habitat: Forested areas, rural area and cultivation.
Diet: Insectivorous. Top prey includes insects like grasshoppers, locusts, etc. Also feed on small mammals including rodents (mice etc.).
